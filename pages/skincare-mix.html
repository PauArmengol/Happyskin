Facial routine for combination skin

Combination skin is characterized by having an area with visible pores and localized oil presence in the well-known T-zone (chin, nose, and forehead), along with other areas of the face having normal or dry skin.

The top 3 points you should consider in your skincare routine are as follows:

Cleansing. Removing dirt from your skin may sound basic, but are you performing your facial cleansing correctly and effectively? It is a crucial step in your routine, as without clean skin, the products you apply afterward will be less effective and may not provide the expected results.

Exfoliation. It involves removing dead skin cells and preventing clogged pores.

Hydration. The skin needs to have the water it requires to help regulate the skin's sebum. Water and sebum are necessary for the skin to function properly. Excess sebum can lead to acne, while a lack of hydration can contribute to the appearance of wrinkles and make your skin look dull and weak.
